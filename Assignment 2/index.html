Allama Muhammad Iqbal

Poet of the East | Thinker | Philosopher

Introduction

Allama Muhammad Iqbal was one of the greatest philosophers, poets, and thinkers of the Muslim world. He is known as the Poet of the East (Shair-e-Mashriq). His poetry awakened the Muslims of the subcontinent and gave them a vision of self-respect, freedom, and unity.

Early Life

Allama Iqbal was born on 9 November 1877 in Sialkot, British India (now Pakistan). He received his early education in Sialkot and later studied in Lahore, England, and Germany. He earned a doctorate in philosophy and became a well-known scholar.

Poetry and Philosophy

Iqbal’s poetry focuses on self-realization (Khudi), faith, courage, and action. He wrote poetry in Urdu and Persian. His famous books include:

  1. Bang-e-Dra
  2. Bal-e-Jibril
  3. Asrar-e-Khudi
  4. Zarb-e-Kalim

His work encouraged Muslims to rise, think independently, and shape their own destiny.

Role in the Creation of Pakistan

Allama Iqbal is considered the ideological founder of Pakistan. In his 1930 Allahabad Address, he presented the idea of a separate homeland for Muslims of the subcontinent. His vision later became a reality under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinna

“Khudi ko kar buland itna ke har taqdeer se pehle, Khuda bande se khud poochay bata teri raza kya hai.”
“Nations are born in the hearts of poets; they prosper and die in the hands of politicians.”
“Failure is not fatal until we surrender.”

Death and Legacy

Allama Iqbal passed away on 21 April 1938, but his thoughts and poetry continue to inspire millions around the world. He is remembered as a leader who awakened the Muslim Ummah and shaped the ideology of Pakistan.
